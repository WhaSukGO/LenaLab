Cross-space generalization (smart-space) — train on UNSEEN warehouses, 2026-06-22
Train: Warehouse_000/001/002/003 (19 cams each, 600 frames). Test: Warehouse_020 (16 cams, UNSEEN).
Scene-agnostic IPM (per-sample cam_proj+cam_valid+grid_bounds, canonical 224x224 grid), from scratch.

RESULT (from-scratch, 4 train scenes): best unseen-warehouse IoU = 0.0370
  vs per-space self-verification (same domain) = ~0.39  -> ~10x drop.
=> A model trained on a few warehouses does NOT transfer to a brand-new warehouse from scratch
   (train loss fell to 0.07 but unseen IoU plateaued ~0.037 = overfitting to the training spaces).
   This is the honest hard finding: cross-space generalization needs far more scenes and/or
   pretrained features. It VALIDATES the per-space self-verification framing (stand up + verify a
   model FOR each space) as the deployable approach, rather than one model for all warehouses.
Checkpoint: artifacts/smartspace/checkpoints/xspace_ref.pt (best) + .full.pt (resume/train-more).

--- ablation (pretrained backbone), 2026-06-22 ---
Same 4-train / unseen-W020 setup, ResNet18 ImageNet-pretrained backbone:
  cross-space from-scratch  unseen IoU = 0.0370
  cross-space pretrained    unseen IoU = 0.0435  (+18% rel, still ~9x below per-space ~0.39)
=> Pretrained features help marginally; cross-space generalization is NOT solved by the backbone.
   The bottleneck is training-space DIVERSITY (only 4 warehouses). Honest conclusion: real
   cross-space transfer needs many more training warehouses (the field uses dozens of scenes) and/or
   per-space test-time adaptation. Per-space self-verification (~0.39) remains the deployable path.
Checkpoints kept: xspace_ref.pt (scratch) + xspace_ref_pretrained.pt (+ .full.pt resume states).

--- 12-train run + per-scene analysis, 2026-06-22 ---
12 train warehouses (W000-011, pretrained backbone, 18 ep) -> best unseen IoU = 0.0457.
Scaling 4->12 train scenes barely moved it (0.044 -> 0.046).

Per-scene breakdown (12-train checkpoint):
  UNSEEN  W020(16cam)=0.046  W021(16cam)=0.036  W022(4cam)=0.072
  TRAIN   W000(19cam)=0.181  W005(19cam)=0.181  W011(10cam)=0.294

DIAGNOSIS — it's a pure TRANSFER failure, not a capacity problem:
- On TRAIN warehouses the shared model scores ~0.18-0.29, i.e. about as well as a DEDICATED
  per-space IPM reference (~0.22). So one model CAN fit many seen warehouses fine.
- On UNSEEN warehouses it collapses to ~0.04-0.07 -> a ~4-5x train->unseen gap. The model
  memorizes each training warehouse's camera geometry/viewpoints; that knowledge does NOT
  transfer to a new space's distinct layout. (W022's 4-cam higher score is a small-sample
  quirk: tiny covered area = sparser/easier grid.)
- Tripling scenes (4->12) didn't help because the issue isn't "too few examples to fit" -
  it's that each warehouse is its own distribution; seen-space fit was already fine.

IMPLICATION: more training scenes alone won't close it. The realistic fix is per-space
ADAPTATION (pretrain a generalist, then briefly fine-tune on a little of the new space) -
which converts the transfer problem into quick specialization, and per-space specialization
already works (per-space ~0.22 IPM / ~0.39 agent). Confirms per-space self-verification as
the deployable path; "one frozen model for all warehouses" does not generalize here.
